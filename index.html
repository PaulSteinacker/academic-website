Welcome

Hello and welcome to my academic website. I am working towards a fully-integrated fault-tolerant silicon quantum computer chip. I specialize in qubit mass characterisation and benchmarking.

This site provides information about my research, teaching, and publications. Feel free to explore and get in touch.

About

I am currently doctoral candidate at UNSW and qubit measurment engineer at Diraq.

My academic interests include quantum computing, neural networks, and financial modelling.

Research

My current research explores industry-compatible silicon metal-oxide-semiconductor qubits based on the electron spin in gate-defined quantum dots. I am particularly interested in facilitating a useful and economical quantum computer.

  • Current Projects: Effects of high-temperature qubit operation, mass qubit characterization, electron shuttling, and auto-tuning of qubit devices.
  • Collaborations: Working with interdisciplinary teams across the broad quantum computing community ranging from auto-tuning over fidelity benchmarking to investigating fundamental sources of noise.
